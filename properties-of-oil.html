8 Properties of Oil

8.1 Properties of Crude Oil Systems

Crude oil systems are complex mixtures of hydrocarbons that exhibit a variety of physical and chemical properties. Understanding these properties is critical for designing and optimizing petroleum production and refining processes. The key properties of crude oil systems include:

8.1.1 Density and Specific Gravity

The density of crude oil is an essential parameter for classification and transport. It is commonly expressed in terms of specific gravity or API gravity, which provides a standardized measurement of how heavy or light the crude oil is compared to water.

8.1.1.1 Crude Oil Gravity

Crude oil gravity is a measure of the oilâ€™s density compared to water, expressed in terms of API gravity. This property helps classify crude oils and provides insight into their quality and composition. Higher API gravity indicates lighter crude oils, which generally contain more valuable fractions like gasoline. Lower API gravity represents heavier crude oils with higher amounts of residual components.

8.1.1.2 Formula for API Gravity

API gravity is calculated using the specific gravity of crude oil at 60Â°F:

\[ \text{API Gravity} = \frac{141.5}{\text{Specific Gravity at 60Â°F}} - 131.5 \]

8.1.1.3 Importance of Crude Oil Gravity

  • Classification: Determines if the crude oil is light, medium, or heavy.
  • Reservoir Management: Influences recovery methods and production strategies.
  • Refining: Lighter oils are easier and more cost-effective to process compared to heavier oils.

Understanding crude oil gravity is fundamental for designing efficient exploration, production, and refining operations.

8.1.1.4 Specific gravity of solution gas

The specific gravity of the solution gas (\(\gamma_g\)) is the ratio of the gas density to the density of air under standard conditions. It is an important property for evaluating the gas-oil ratio (GOR) and understanding reservoir fluid behavior.

The specific gravity of the solution gas can be calculated using the following formula:

\[ \gamma_g = \frac{\sum (y_i \cdot \gamma_{gi})}{\sum y_i} \]

where: - \(y_i\): Mole fraction of the \(i\)-th component in the gas. - \(\gamma_{gi}\): Specific gravity of the \(i\)-th component with respect to air.

This calculation requires the composition of the gas mixture, including the specific gravities and mole fractions of its components.

8.1.2 Viscosity

Viscosity reflects the internal resistance of crude oil to flow. It is a critical factor in determining the ease of transport through pipelines and the efficiency of production from reservoirs.

8.1.3 Composition

Crude oil is primarily composed of hydrocarbons, including paraffins, naphthenes, and aromatics, along with trace amounts of sulfur, nitrogen, and oxygen compounds. The composition affects the behavior and value of the oil.

8.1.4 Solution Gas-Oil Ratio (GOR)

This property represents the volume of gas dissolved in crude oil at reservoir conditions. The GOR plays a significant role in reservoir performance and surface facility design.

Gas solubility, denoted as \(R_s\), refers to the volume of gas that dissolves in one stock-tank barrel (STB) of crude oil under specific pressure and temperature conditions. This property is crucial for evaluating reservoir performance and production strategies.

8.1.4.1 Key Concepts:

  1. Pressure Dependency:
    • \(R_s\) increases with pressure until reaching the bubble-point pressure.
    • At pressures below the bubble point, gas begins to liberate from the oil, reducing \(R_s\).
  2. Factors Influencing Gas Solubility:
    • Reservoir temperature
    • API gravity of the crude oil
    • Specific gravity of the gas

8.1.5 Bubble Point Pressure

The bubble point pressure is the pressure at which gas begins to come out of solution from crude oil. This property is crucial for reservoir management and production strategies.

8.1.6 Formation Volume Factor

The formation volume factor relates the reservoir volume of oil to its volume at standard conditions. It accounts for the effects of pressure, temperature, and dissolved gas on crude oil volume.

8.1.7 Compressibility

Oil compressibility describes the change in crude oil volume with changes in pressure and temperature. It impacts reservoir modeling and simulation efforts.

8.1.8 Thermal Properties

The thermal properties of crude oil, such as heat capacity and thermal conductivity, influence heat transfer during production, transport, and refining processes.

By studying these properties, engineers can better predict the behavior of crude oil under various conditions and optimize production, processing, and transportation methods.

8.2 Empirical correlations

8.2.1 Vasquez and Beggs Correlations

8.2.1.1 Bubble Point Pressure

The bubble point pressure is given by:

\[ p_{bp} = \left( \frac{R_{so}}{C_1 G \cdot e^{C_3 \left(\frac{\gamma_o}{T + 460}\right)}} \right)^{\frac{1}{C_2}} \]

where:

Coefficient \(\gamma_o \leq 30^\circ\) API \(\gamma_o > 30^\circ\) API
\(C_1\) 0.0362 0.0178
\(C_2\) 1.0937 1.1870
\(C_3\) 25.7240 23.9310

8.2.1.2 Solution Gas-Oil Ratio

The solution gas-oil ratio is expressed as:

\[ R_{so} = C_1 G p^{C_2} \cdot e^{C_3 \left(\frac{\gamma_o}{T + 460}\right)} \]

8.2.1.3 Oil Formation Volume Factor (FVF) â€“ Saturated

\[ B_o = 1 + A_1 R_{so} + A_2 \left( T - 60 \right) \left( \frac{\gamma_o}{G} \right) + A_3 R_{so} \left( T - 60 \right) \left( \frac{\gamma_o}{G} \right) \]

Coefficient \(\gamma_o \leq 30^\circ\) API \(\gamma_o > 30^\circ\) API
\(A_1\) 4.677E-04 4.670E-04
\(A_2\) 1.751E-05 1.100E-05
\(A_3\) -1.811E-08 1.337E-09

8.2.1.4 Oil FVF â€“ Undersaturated

\[ B_o = B_{ob} \cdot e^{c_o \left(p_{bp} - p \right)} \]

Where: - \(B_{ob}\): Formation volume factor at the bubble point pressure - \(c_o\): Oil compressibility

8.2.1.5 Compressibility â€“ Saturated

\[ c_o = \frac{-1433 + 5 R_{sob} + 17.2 T - 1180 G + 12.61 \gamma_o}{10^5 p} + \frac{B_g}{B_o} \cdot \frac{dR_{so}}{dp} \cdot \frac{1}{5.615} \]

Where: - \(R_{sob}\): Solution gas-oil ratio at the bubble point pressure

8.2.1.6 Compressibility â€“ Undersaturated

\[ c_o = \frac{-1433 + 5 R_{so} + 17.2 T - 1180 G + 12.61 \gamma_o}{10^5 p} \]

import math

# Function to calculate bubble point pressure
def bubble_point_pressure(R_so, G, gamma_o, T, C1, C2, C3):
    """
    Calculate bubble point pressure using the Vasquez and Beggs correlation.
    
    Parameters:
    R_so: Solution gas-oil ratio (scf/STB)
    G: Gas specific gravity (dimensionless)
    gamma_o: Oil API gravity (dimensionless)
    T: Temperature in Â°F
    C1, C2, C3: Correlation coefficients based on API
    
    Returns:
    p_bp: Bubble point pressure (psia)
    """
    exponent = C3 * (gamma_o / (T + 460))
    denominator = C1 * G * math.exp(exponent)
    p_bp = (R_so / denominator) ** (1 / C2)
    return p_bp

# Function to calculate solution gas-oil ratio
def solution_gas_oil_ratio(C1, G, p, C2, C3, gamma_o, T):
    """
    Calculate solution gas-oil ratio using the Vasquez and Beggs correlation.
    
    Parameters:
    C1, C2, C3: Correlation coefficients based on API
    G: Gas specific gravity (dimensionless)
    p: Pressure in psia
    gamma_o: Oil API gravity (dimensionless)
    T: Temperature in Â°F
    
    Returns:
    R_so: Solution gas-oil ratio (scf/STB)
    """
    exponent = C3 * (gamma_o / (T + 460))
    R_so = C1 * G * (p ** C2) * math.exp(exponent)
    return R_so

import math

# Function to calculate saturated oil FVF
def oil_fvf_saturated(R_so, T, gamma_o, G, A1, A2, A3):
    """
    Calculate saturated oil FVF using the Vasquez and Beggs correlation.

    Parameters:
    R_so: Solution gas-oil ratio (scf/STB)
    T: Temperature in Â°F
    gamma_o: Oil API gravity
    G: Gas specific gravity
    A1, A2, A3: Correlation coefficients based on API

    Returns:
    B_o: Oil FVF (bbl/STB)
    """
    term1 = A1 * R_so
    term2 = A2 * (T - 60) * (gamma_o / G)
    term3 = A3 * R_so * (T - 60) * (gamma_o / G)
    B_o = 1 + term1 + term2 + term3
    return B_o

# Function to calculate undersaturated oil FVF
def oil_fvf_undersaturated(B_ob, c_o, p_bp, p):
    """
    Calculate undersaturated oil FVF using the Vasquez and Beggs correlation.

    Parameters:
    B_ob: Oil FVF at the bubble point pressure (bbl/STB)
    c_o: Oil compressibility (psi^-1)
    p_bp: Bubble point pressure (psia)
    p: Pressure (psia)

    Returns:
    B_o: Oil FVF (bbl/STB)
    """
    B_o = B_ob * math.exp(c_o * (p_bp - p))
    return B_o

# Function to calculate compressibility for saturated oil
def compressibility_saturated(R_sob, T, G, gamma_o, p, B_g, B_o, dR_so_dp):
    """
    Calculate oil compressibility for saturated oil.

    Parameters:
    R_sob: Solution gas-oil ratio at the bubble point pressure (scf/STB)
    T: Temperature in Â°F
    G: Gas specific gravity
    gamma_o: Oil API gravity
    p: Pressure in psia
    B_g: Gas formation volume factor (bbl/scf)
    B_o: Oil formation volume factor (bbl/STB)
    dR_so_dp: Derivative of solution gas-oil ratio with respect to pressure (scf/STB/psi)

    Returns:
    c_o: Oil compressibility (psi^-1)
    """
    term1 = (-1433 + 5 * R_sob + 17.2 * T - 1180 * G + 12.61 * gamma_o) / (10**5 * p)
    term2 = (B_g / B_o) * (dR_so_dp / 5.615)
    c_o = term1 + term2
    return c_o

# Function to calculate compressibility for undersaturated oil
def compressibility_undersaturated(R_so, T, G, gamma_o, p):
    """
    Calculate oil compressibility for undersaturated oil.

    Parameters:
    R_so: Solution gas-oil ratio (scf/STB)
    T: Temperature in Â°F
    G: Gas specific gravity
    gamma_o: Oil API gravity
    p: Pressure in psia

    Returns:
    c_o: Oil compressibility (psi^-1)
    """
    c_o = (-1433 + 5 * R_so + 17.2 * T - 1180 * G + 12.61 * gamma_o) / (10**5 * p)
    return c_o
